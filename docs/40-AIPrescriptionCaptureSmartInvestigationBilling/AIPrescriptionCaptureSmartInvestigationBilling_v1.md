## MODULE 40: AI Prescription Capture & Smart Investigation Billing

### 1. Executive Summary
The AI Prescription Capture & Smart Investigation Billing module transforms unstructured, handwritten, or digital prescriptions into structured, actionable healthcare data. By utilizing advanced Optical Character Recognition (OCR) and Artificial Intelligence (AI), the system automatically extracts patient details, medications, investigations, and clinical notes. It intelligently maps extracted tests to the hospital's Master Service Catalog to generate precise billing recommendations and investigation orders, drastically reducing manual data entry while maintaining strict human-in-the-loop verification for clinical safety.

### 2. Business Purpose
To eliminate the bottleneck at the billing and reception desks caused by manually deciphering and typing complex prescriptions. This module reduces transcription errors, prevents revenue leakage through smart billing recommendations, avoids duplicate testing, and accelerates the patient's journey from consultation to laboratory/pharmacy execution.

### 3. Actors
*   **Patient**: Uploads external prescriptions via the Patient Portal.
*   **Reception Officer**: Scans or photographs physical prescriptions at the front desk.
*   **Billing Officer**: Reviews AI billing recommendations and finalizes invoices.
*   **Lab Reception**: Receives structured investigation orders generated by the AI.
*   **Doctor**: Views digitized historical prescriptions.
*   **Telemedicine Coordinator**: Imports prescriptions from virtual consults.
*   **AI Verification Officer**: A specialized role (often a nurse or pharmacist) to manually verify low-confidence AI extractions.
*   **Medical Records Officer**: Audits the accuracy of digitized records.
*   **Hospital Administrator**: Monitors AI accuracy and operational throughput.
*   **System Administrator**: Manages AI models, mappings, and system configurations.

### 4. Functional Requirements

#### 1. Prescription Image Capture
*   **Support**: Mobile photo upload, scanner upload, PDF upload, Patient Portal upload, WhatsApp import (future), Email import (future).
*   **Supported Formats**: JPG, PNG, PDF, TIFF.

#### 2. AI Prescription Recognition
*   **Extraction Targets**: Doctor name, Registration number, Prescription date, Patient name, Age, Gender, Medicines, Investigations, Diagnoses, Clinical notes, Follow-up date.
*   **Confidence Tracking**: Every extracted entity must be assigned an AI confidence score (0-100%).

#### 3. Human Verification Workflow
*   **Statuses**: `Uploaded` → `AI Processing` → `AI Parsed` → `Verification Pending` → `Verified` / `Rejected` → `Archived`.
*   **Rule**: Items falling below a configured confidence threshold (e.g., < 85%) require mandatory manual confirmation by the AI Verification Officer.

#### 4. Smart Investigation Mapping
*   **Mapping**: Translates extracted text to the Master Service Catalog (Laboratory, Radiology, Procedures).
*   **NLP Handling**: Resolves synonyms, abbreviations, and alternate spellings (e.g., "CBC" → "Complete Blood Count", "FBS" → "Fasting Blood Sugar", "CXR" → "Chest X-Ray").

#### 5. Smart Billing Recommendation
*   **Generation**: Automatically compiles suggested investigations, suggested billing packages, and an estimated total cost.
*   **Flexibility**: Allows the Billing Officer to manually adjust, add, or remove items before final invoice generation.

#### 6. Duplicate Detection
*   **Detection**: Identifies duplicate investigations within the same upload, duplicate uploads of the same physical paper, and recent existing orders for the same patient to prevent unnecessary billing and clinical redundancy.

#### 7. Medicine Recognition
*   **Extraction**: Brand name, Generic name, Strength, Dose, Frequency, Duration.
*   **Integration**: Maps to Module 20 (Pharmacy & Medication Catalog).

#### 8. Investigation Order Generation
*   **Output**: Creates structured orders integrating directly with Module 21 (Sample Collection), Module 22 (Result Entry), Module 23 (Verification), and Module 24 (Report Release).

#### 9. Clinical Coding Readiness
*   **Mapping**: Prepares extracted diagnoses for mapping to ICD-10, and future readiness for ICD-11 and SNOMED CT.

#### 10. AI Learning Feedback Loop
*   **Capture**: Logs the original AI prediction, the human correction, and the final accepted value to support future model retraining and continuous improvement.

#### 11. Multi-Language Prescription Support
*   **Recognition**: Supports English, Bangla, Arabic, Urdu, and mixed-language prescriptions (e.g., English drug names with Arabic instructions).

#### 12. Telemedicine Integration
*   **Import**: Seamlessly ingests prescriptions generated in Module 32 or from future external telemedicine providers.

#### 13. Patient Portal Integration
*   **Upload**: Patients can upload prescriptions via the portal (and future mobile app) and track the processing status (e.g., "Pending Billing Estimate").

#### 14. AI Alert Engine
*   **Detection**: Flags unclear prescriptions, missing patient details, duplicate test requests, suspicious drug combinations, or missing doctor signatures.

#### 15. Bulk Processing
*   **Support**: Batch scanning and processing for diagnostic camps or corporate health checkups.

### 5. Non-Functional Requirements
*   **Multi-tenant Isolation**: All images, extractions, and mappings are strictly scoped by `CompanyId`.
*   **Localization**: UI supports EN, BN, AR, UR, HI with RTL rendering. No hardcoded UI text.
*   **Future Readiness**: Architecture supports ASP.NET WebForms now, with clear separation for future MVC/Core/API migration.

### 9. Acceptance Criteria
*   **OCR Accuracy**: The system successfully extracts printed text with >95% accuracy and handwritten text with >80% accuracy (dependent on model).
*   **Human Verification Workflow**: The system hard-blocks billing order generation if any extracted item falls below the tenant-configured confidence threshold until manually verified.
*   **Billing Safety**: The AI can only *recommend* a bill; a human Billing Officer must explicitly click "Accept & Generate Invoice".
*   **Laboratory Integration**: Accepted investigation mappings automatically populate the Laboratory Order queue (Module 21).
*   **Auditability**: Every human override of an AI prediction is permanently logged in `AIFeedbackLearning` and `AIPrescriptionAudit`.
*   **Multi-tenancy**: AI mappings, uploaded images, and patient data are strictly isolated by `CompanyId`.
*   **Localization**: The OCR engine and UI support processing and displaying Arabic/Urdu (RTL) alongside English/Bangla (LTR).
*   **API Readiness**: The core extraction logic is decoupled, allowing future mobile apps to send an image payload and receive structured JSON back.

### Appendix A – Enterprise Enhancements

#### 1. LLM-Assisted Prescription Interpretation
*   **Architecture**: Future integration with Large Language Models (e.g., GPT-4 / Med-PaLM) to interpret complex, unstructured clinical notes and infer missing context (e.g., deducing the route of administration from context if not explicitly written).

#### 2. Handwriting Recognition Models
*   **Architecture**: Deployment of specialized, medical-domain HTR (Handwritten Text Recognition) neural networks fine-tuned specifically on doctors' cursive handwriting styles prevalent in the operating region.

#### 3. Clinical Decision Support (CDS) Integration
*   **Architecture**: Passing the AI-extracted medications and diagnoses through a CDS engine to flag potential drug-drug interactions or contraindications before the prescription is finalized in the hospital's system.

#### 4. AI Investigation Recommendation Engine
*   **Architecture**: Based on the extracted diagnoses (e.g., "Type 2 Diabetes"), the AI suggests additional standard-of-care investigations (e.g., HbA1c, Lipid Panel) that the doctor may have missed, prompting the billing officer to inquire.

#### 5. AI Package Recommendation Engine
*   **Architecture**: If the AI detects multiple individual tests (e.g., CBC, Lipid Profile, LFT, RFT), it automatically recommends converting the bill to a pre-configured "Comprehensive Health Check Package" to offer the patient a better rate and simplify billing.

#### 6. Voice Prescription Capture
*   **Architecture**: Future module allowing doctors to dictate prescriptions via mobile app, using NLP to instantly structure the audio into the `AIPrescriptionCapture` schema.

#### 7. Real-Time Mobile Camera OCR
*   **Architecture**: Edge-AI integration in the future mobile app, providing real-time bounding boxes and text extraction overlay on the smartphone camera *before* the user even snaps the photo, ensuring optimal image quality.

#### 8. National ePrescription Integration
*   **Architecture**: Ability to ingest structured XML/JSON prescriptions directly from national health gateways, bypassing OCR entirely and feeding directly into the smart billing engine.

#### 9. FHIR-Based AI Interoperability
*   **Architecture**: Structuring the AI output into standard FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) resources (e.g., `MedicationRequest`, `ServiceRequest`) to ensure seamless data exchange with external EMRs.

#### 10. Federated Learning Across Tenants
*   **Architecture**: A privacy-preserving machine learning architecture where the AI model improves by learning from human corrections across multiple hospital tenants (CompanyIds) without ever sharing or centralizing the underlying Protected Health Information (PHI).

### Appendix B – Enterprise AI Governance & Clinical Safety Enhancements

#### 1. AI Model Governance
*   **Overview**: Strict lifecycle management for the underlying machine learning models to ensure regulatory compliance.
*   **Capabilities**: Track Model name, Model version, Training date, Deployment date, Retirement date, and Validation status. Supports full model auditability if an extraction error leads to a clinical incident.

#### 2. AI Decision Explainability
*   **Overview**: "White-box" AI approach ensuring humans can trace the logic behind automated decisions.
*   **Capabilities**: For every prediction, the system stores the Confidence score, Supporting evidence (e.g., bounding box coordinates on the image), Mapping rationale, and the final Verification result. Allows reviewers to understand exactly *why* the AI made a specific recommendation.

#### 3. Clinical Risk Classification
*   **Overview**: Dynamic routing of verification tasks based on the potential clinical impact of an error.
*   **Capabilities**: Classify extraction outcomes into Low Risk (e.g., Vitamin supplements), Moderate Risk, High Risk, and Critical Risk (e.g., Oncology drugs, High-alert medications). Critical items require mandatory, un-skippable human review regardless of the AI's confidence score.

#### 4. AI Performance Monitoring
*   **Overview**: Executive dashboards tracking the operational ROI and safety of the AI deployment.
*   **KPIs**: OCR accuracy, Extraction accuracy, Mapping accuracy, Verification rejection rate, Human correction rate, and Processing time (TAT).

#### 5. Drift Detection Readiness
*   **Overview**: Automated monitoring to detect when the AI model's performance begins to degrade over time.
*   **Capabilities**: Prepare future monitoring for Accuracy drift, Language drift (e.g., new slang or abbreviations), Specialty drift, and Doctor handwriting drift (e.g., a new doctor joins with highly illegible handwriting causing a localized spike in error rates).

#### 6. Clinical Safety Alerts
*   **Overview**: Rule-based safety nets that catch dangerous or incomplete prescriptions before they reach the pharmacy or lab.
*   **Capabilities**: Detect Ambiguous medicine names (e.g., look-alike/sound-alike drugs), Ambiguous investigation names, Missing dosage, Missing frequency, Missing duration, and Missing doctor identity.

#### 7. Regulatory Compliance Readiness
*   **Overview**: Architectural alignment with emerging global standards for Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) and AI in healthcare.
*   **Capabilities**: Prepare for National AI regulations, Medical device regulations, Healthcare AI audits, and strict Data privacy regulations (HIPAA/GDPR) regarding the storage of un-redacted prescription images.

#### 8. AI Review Committee Workflow
*   **Overview**: A structured governance workflow for approving new AI models or major mapping updates.
*   **Capabilities**: Support future governance by requiring sign-offs from a Clinical reviewer, Laboratory reviewer, Pharmacy reviewer, and Quality reviewer before a new AI version goes live. Tracks the complete approval history.

#### 9. AI Incident Management
*   **Overview**: A dedicated module for logging and investigating failures in the AI pipeline.
*   **Capabilities**: Capture AI errors, Near misses, Incorrect recommendations, Incorrect mappings, and Clinical safety events. Supports corrective action tracking (CAPA) to retrain the model or adjust the UI.

#### 10. Enterprise AI Dashboard
*   **Overview**: A high-level BI view for hospital leadership to measure the value of the AI module.
*   **Capabilities**: Display AI usage volume, Accuracy trends, Model performance, Error trends, Verification workload, Revenue impact (e.g., missed tests caught by AI), and Total time savings (FTE hours saved).
